UNIVERSITY STUDENT HANDBOOK — Academic Year 2024-2025

SECTION 1: ATTENDANCE POLICY
Students are expected to attend at least 75% of all scheduled classes. Attendance below 75% may result in the student being barred from appearing in final examinations. Medical absences must be supported by a certified doctor's note submitted within 5 working days of the absence.

SECTION 2: ASSIGNMENT SUBMISSION POLICY
All assignments must be submitted via the official student portal by 11:59 PM on the due date. Assignments submitted after the deadline will incur a 10% penalty per day late. No submission will be accepted after 5 days past the deadline. Extensions may be granted only on medical or emergency grounds with prior written approval from the department head.

SECTION 3: GRADING SCALE
A+: 95–100 | A: 90–94 | A-: 85–89
B+: 80–84  | B: 75–79 | B-: 70–74
C+: 65–69  | C: 60–64 | F: Below 60
Students receiving an F must repeat the course. The minimum passing grade for core modules is C (60%).

SECTION 4: ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
Plagiarism, cheating, or any form of academic dishonesty is strictly prohibited. First offence: written warning and zero on the assessment. Second offence: suspension for one semester. Third offence: permanent expulsion from the university. All written work will be screened using anti-plagiarism software.

SECTION 5: EXAMINATION REGULATIONS
Students must carry their valid student ID to all examinations. Electronic devices including mobile phones and smartwatches are strictly prohibited in the examination hall. Calculators are only permitted if explicitly stated on the exam paper. Students arriving more than 30 minutes late will not be permitted to enter.

SECTION 6: LIBRARY SERVICES
The university library is open Monday to Friday 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM, and Saturday 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM. Students may borrow up to 5 books at a time for a period of 14 days. Overdue fines are charged at Rs. 5 per day per book. Inter-library loans can be requested through the library portal with a processing time of 3–5 business days.

SECTION 7: STUDENT SUPPORT SERVICES
Counselling services are available free of charge to all enrolled students. Appointments can be booked online or by visiting Room 204, Administrative Block. The university also offers a peer-mentoring programme pairing first-year students with senior mentors. Career services including resume reviews and mock interviews are available at the Career Centre, Building C.

SECTION 8: FEE PAYMENT POLICY
Tuition fees must be paid in full before the semester begins or through the approved installment plan. Students with outstanding fees after the 30-day grace period will have their portal access suspended until payment is cleared. Scholarships and financial aid forms must be submitted by March 31st for the following academic year.
